About umwero



Before inventing this alphabet I was facing some challenges or problems in writing of Kinyarwanda language. First, I meet those challenges when I was with inspiration to write some songs or poem in a different time continuously. Like when I was saying R(u)G(u)WA for maybe RGWANDA I was writing it as R(u)w(A) for Rwanda which is totally different in the way we speak it; other example is ligature TW if you read this gihekane as it is written it will sound as T(u)WA because is Ta+Wa but in good spoken Kinyarwanda we say as T(u)K(u)WA for some word like UGU T(u)K(u)WI there I was found that in all ligatures (ibihekane) there is a missing sound.

One day after finishing my secondary study, I was at Nairobi Kenya in vacation and I found that they write “CH” where we write only “C”(but we speak that sound the same.) by example in swahili they write “changa moto” and us we write “cana itara” and also in the origin of that Latin letter C it sound as “SI” or “se”. on that case I found that the Latin alphabet cannot and will not fit any of the Bantu language completely as it sounds.

I found that the bantu languages sound the same but written in different ways because of Latin alphabet we use which is not compatible with our sound speaking at all.

After my vacation I returned at home to study at university of Rwanda in computer science with those writing challenges I have faced before visiting Kenya and with those writing challenges I have faced in Kenya. At that time, I was started to wish that the bantu languages should have their writing system.

Back in the days in 2014, the writing of Kinyarwanda have been changed and that change was a great conflict among citizen also those changes have been corrected in 2020 but all those conflicts and changes are because of Latinization of our language, here I may say that it is wrong to assign one letter to different sound that why I may say: those change and their corrections are all wrong because by example we assign letter K to Q[iya] for sounding Kigali as like icyibo and assign K to K[ah] for sounding kanombe , so if you listen correctly you may hear there are different sound that why they have to have different letter for each sound, I mean letter for Q[iya] and other for k[ah]. .

The reason behind all of this conflict and changes it is in history because we are taught to write Kinyarwanda by our colonizer who come to us without knowing our language. So, I would ask you as I always was ask myself, how can someone who doesn’t know to speak your language can teach you to write your language? Mission impossible. That why my wish was to develop our own writing system.

On my research I have found that the Kinyarwanda language is a language spoken by all Rwandan as easy spoken language but I found that it is very hard to write it to all Rwandans. Many Rwandan from primary to secondary the Kinyarwanda script challenge them especially in writing of Kinyarwanda ligatures (ibihekane). But the problem is not in IBIHEKANE only but also, we use a character of different sound as a sound of Kinyarwanda example we use letter C which sound originally like si and we use it as sound cah of our language. We use vowel E which sound originally like ih and we use it as sound eh of our language and also in latin alphabet of our language we write some sound as it is igihekane(ligature)but it is not if you listen correctly you may hear it as a single sound I mean only 1 letter .

I have examine many people of different generation by telling them like please write for me IGIHEKANE shy (not only this, it is random example) but many of them fail and those write as we studied they start to think if what they think is correct or not means they write it without a confidence. Because we write as not, we speak. So if we write as we speak, to write any character or IBIHEKANE it will not be something to think about.

If you listen on that SHY it is only a single character not combined characters or ligature

Umwero means a harvest time so, it is a harvest for us to raise our language from vanishing to protection and also is the best step to strengthen our national identity.



Some of umwero symbols are based on our culture example is it letter for sound AH(a) which looks like a cow’s head as you know cows was one among the pillars of ancient Rwanda kingdom (Kalinga)

The artistic principle to develop each character was based on the middle joint of every glyph to connect them all; on calligraph is based on like 7cm and the joint is located on third cm (joint is shown by that arrow shape).
